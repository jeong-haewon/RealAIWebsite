NutriBench: Evaluating LLMs on Nutrition Estimation

NUTRIBENCH is the first publicly available dataset of 11,857 human-verified meal descriptions annotated with macronutrient labels, designed to evaluate large language models (LLMs) on nutrition estimation. We benchmark twelve LLMs, compare their performance to professional nutritionists, and assess real-world health impacts through blood glucose management simulations in individuals with diabetes.

Read Paper GitHub Dataset
Simulated blood glucose traces comparing GPT-4o estimates vs. nutritionist estimates for diabetes management.

The Problem

Accurate carbohydrate estimation is critical for individuals with diabetes who must calculate insulin doses based on meal content. However, nutrition estimation is challenging even for trained professionals, and errors can lead to dangerous blood glucose fluctuations.

Can large language models provide accurate, fast nutrition estimates that improve diabetes management? NutriBench provides the first comprehensive benchmark to answer this question.

Dataset Overview

NutriBench contains 11,857 human-verified meal descriptions with macronutrient annotations, representing diverse global dietary patterns.

Global Coverage

Data sourced from dietary intake databases spanning 11 countries:

Argentina Bulgaria Ethiopia India Italy Mexico Nigeria Peru Philippines Sri Lanka United States

Data Sources

  • What We Eat in America (WWEIA) - National dietary intake survey
  • FAO/WHO Gift databases - Global dietary data from multiple countries

Version 2 expanded coverage to 24 countries with improved meal diversity.

Methodology

Data Generation Process

  1. Meal data extracted from real dietary intake databases
  2. GPT-4o-mini used to generate natural language descriptions
  3. Human verification conducted to eliminate hallucinations

LLM Evaluation

We benchmarked 12 models across different categories:

Category Models
Open-source Llama 3.1 (8B/70B/405B), Llama 3 (8B/70B), Gemma 2 (9B/27B), Qwen 2 (7B/70B)
Closed-source GPT-4o, GPT-4o mini
Medical-specialized OpenBioLLM-70B

Prompting Strategies

Four prompting approaches were tested:

  1. Baseline - Standard instructional prompting
  2. Chain-of-Thought (CoT) - Step-by-step reasoning
  3. Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) - Database-enhanced responses
  4. RAG+CoT - Combined approach

Key Results

GPT-4o with Chain-of-Thought achieved 66.82% accuracy (absolute error <7g carbohydrates) with a 99.16% answer rate, providing more accurate and faster predictions than professional nutritionists.

Clinical Impact Simulation

We assessed real-world health impacts through blood glucose management simulations:

  • 44,800 scenarios simulated using Type 1 diabetes models
  • GPT-4o estimates resulted in lowest blood glucose risk
  • Achieved highest time in safe glucose range (70-180 mg/dl)
  • Outperformed nutritionist estimates in diabetes management simulations

Recent Updates
